## Problem / why this was next

Pool-disjoint route sets were structural proposals only. Milestone 5 required exact allocation and split replay, a safe no-split fallback, deterministic equal and greedy baselines, an exhaustive tiny allocation oracle before greedy work, and evidence that failures and work limits cannot corrupt the incumbent.

## Decision or implementation

Represent executable splits as canonical positive bigint legs whose allocations sum exactly to the request. Replay every leg from the captured original snapshot and expose a receipt only after all legs succeed. Start from an exact single-path fallback, evaluate canonical equal allocation, and let a bounded greedy policy score exact chunk assignments while reserving authorization for a distinct fresh full-input replay. Keep fallback, structural, allocation, and work-limit accounting explicit and compare plans under exact output, leg count, total hops, route keys, and allocation-vector ties.

## Result

RouteLab now supports bounded exact-input allocation across canonical pool-disjoint routes with exact no-split, equal, and chunk-greedy policies. Every returned plan consumes the exact input and is authorized by fresh exact replay. Independent tiny exhaustive comparisons bound quality claims, preserve a zero-output activation counterexample, and confirm the named unit-chunk optimum and allocation-vector tie. A cumulative read-only review found every Milestone 5 clause complete.

## Limitations / what remains unimplemented

- Routing remains bounded to enumerated pool-disjoint route sets, configured greedy parts, and explicit work limits; no global route or allocation optimality is claimed.
- Integer flooring and zero-output eligibility can make unit-chunk greedy allocation miss the tiny exhaustive optimum.
- Exhaustive optimality evidence is deliberately tiny; very large integer evidence proves exact reconstruction and replay rather than unrestricted quality.
- No approximate allocation model, historical dataset, acceleration layer, service, protocol adapter, transaction submission, custody, or learned ordering is implemented.
